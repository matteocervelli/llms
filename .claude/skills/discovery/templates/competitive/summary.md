# Competitive Landscape

Map what exists before building something new.

## 1. Identify Alternatives

Users with your problem aren't waiting — they're solving it somehow:

- **Direct competitors**: Same problem, similar solution
- **Indirect competitors**: Different approach, same outcome (e.g., spreadsheet instead of app)
- **Do-nothing**: Status quo — what's the cost of inaction for users?

## 2. What Exists Already?

For each alternative, note:

- Why users adopt it
- Why users leave it (or why they'd leave it for you)
- Price point / business model

## 3. Where's the Gap?

Your differentiator must be one of:

- **Better**: Meaningfully better at the core job (not marginally)
- **Cheaper**: Accessible to a segment that can't afford alternatives
- **Different**: Serves a segment the competition ignores or treats as secondary

Avoid "better AND cheaper AND different" — that's a positioning failure, not a win.

## Output

Fill this in before running `/discovery feasibility`:

```
Primary alternative: [name + why users use it]
Their weakness: [what they don't do well]
Your angle: [better / cheaper / different — one only]
Moat hypothesis: [why would this be hard to copy in 12 months?]
```
