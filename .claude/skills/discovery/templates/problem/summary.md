# Problem Framing

Answer three questions before anything else:

## 1. Who has this problem?

- **Primary segment**: Who specifically? (role, context, company size, industry)
- **Secondary segments**: Who else might benefit, but isn't the core target?
- **Anti-segments**: Who does NOT have this problem — and why do they matter to exclude?

## 2. How urgent is it?

Rate urgency on two axes:

- **Frequency**: How often does the problem occur? (daily / weekly / monthly / rare)
- **Pain level**: What happens if it goes unsolved? (blocked / slowed / annoyed / inconvenient)

High urgency = high frequency + high pain. Low urgency = feature request, not a problem.

## 3. What evidence do you have?

- **Observed**: User interviews, support tickets, lost deals, user research
- **Inferred**: Analogous markets, adjacent problems, your own experience
- **Assumed**: Gut feel, hypothesis (mark these clearly)

## Output

Fill this in before running `/discovery competitive`:

```
Segment: [who]
Frequency: [daily/weekly/monthly/rare]
Pain level: [blocked/slowed/annoyed/inconvenient]
Evidence type: [observed/inferred/assumed]
One-line problem statement: [when X happens, Y struggle with Z]
```
